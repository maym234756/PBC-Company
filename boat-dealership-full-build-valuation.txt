Boat Dealership Full-Build Valuation Projection
Prepared: 2026-05-20

Project assessed:
Boat Dealership Cloud Operating Diagram / Builder / Flow Map platform

Important note:
This is a strategic operator estimate, not a formal CPA appraisal, fairness opinion, tax valuation, or investment banking opinion. It is meant to answer a practical business question: if this project were fully built into a production-grade platform, what would it likely cost to finish, and what could it reasonably be worth.

--------------------------------------------------
1. Executive Summary
--------------------------------------------------

Best single-number base case:
- Estimated full production build cost: about $3.07M
- Reasonable strategic asset value once fully built, even before major scale: about $3.5M to $6.5M
- Reasonable value with early traction and paying customers: about $8M to $18M
- Strong upside if it becomes a sticky niche platform in marine dealership operations: about $20M to $45M

If someone asked for the most defensible short answer:
- Full cost to build this into a serious production platform: roughly $2.0M to $4.8M
- What it could be worth if fully built out and positioned correctly: roughly $3M to $6.5M before strong revenue, and materially more once recurring revenue is proven

--------------------------------------------------
2. What Exists Today
--------------------------------------------------

The current asset is more than a static diagram. It already demonstrates a product direction and a domain model.

Current project characteristics observed from the working files:
- Interactive live operating canvas
- Builder controls for adding sections and flows
- Search and inspector behavior
- Quick-focus operating modes
- Separate top-down flow map page
- Export paths to Mermaid source, JSON, PDF, and SVG
- Browser-local persistence for graph state, descriptions, positions, viewport, and sidebar state
- Ctrl/Cmd multi-select and grouped drag behavior

Current modeled scope from the latest export:
- 14 groups
- 236 nodes
- 787 edges
- 236 descriptions
- 236 stored node positions

What this means from a valuation standpoint:
- The project already contains real product thinking, not just design mockups
- The domain modeling work is significant and would take time to recreate
- The front-end prototype reduces discovery risk for a future build
- The asset is still not a production SaaS platform yet because it does not show a live backend, tenant model, enterprise auth, live connectors, billing, observability, or customer deployment workflows

--------------------------------------------------
3. What "Fully Built Out" Means
--------------------------------------------------

For valuation purposes, "fully built out" should mean all of the following are in place:

- Production backend and persistent database
- Multi-user authentication and role-based permissions
- Multi-tenant support for different dealership groups, stores, marinas, and operating units
- Real integrations to CRM, DMS, ERP, lenders, payment systems, reservation systems, parts sources, and notification platforms
- Workflow automation and alerting rules
- KPI, analytics, dashboard, and reporting layers
- Admin console, audit history, backups, and operational controls
- Security hardening, logging, monitoring, and release management
- Documentation, onboarding flow, deployment process, and customer support readiness

If those layers are not built, the project should be valued closer to premium prototype / IP value, not full-platform value.

--------------------------------------------------
4. Valuation Framework
--------------------------------------------------

This project can be valued in three different ways.

1. Replacement cost value
This asks: what would it cost to build this properly from scratch today with a capable product team?

2. Strategic asset value
This asks: what would a buyer pay for the IP, domain model, product direction, and time saved, even before major revenue exists?

3. Commercial platform value
This asks: what is it worth once recurring revenue, customer retention, and deployment repeatability are proven?

The safest way to answer your question is to use all three lenses together.

--------------------------------------------------
8. Revenue Model Assumptions for a Commercial Product
--------------------------------------------------

The clearest path is not to sell this as a diagram tool. The stronger path is to position it as a vertical operating system / workflow intelligence platform for marine dealerships, marinas, service operations, brokerage, storage, rental, and retention programs.

Practical commercial pricing assumptions:
- Enterprise setup / onboarding fee: $75,000 to $250,000
- Annual software contract for smaller groups: $60,000 to $120,000
- Annual software contract for multi-store or more operationally complex groups: $120,000 to $250,000
- Strategic enterprise account with heavy integrations and reporting: $250,000 to $500,000+ annually

The highest-value version is the one that becomes operationally embedded in:
- lead handling
- service capacity control
- title / funding / payoff management
- marina yield and waterfront revenue
- owner retention and upgrade triggers
- executive KPI and exception management

That kind of operational stickiness is what creates valuation, not the visualization alone.

--------------------------------------------------
9. Strategic Asset Value If Fully Built But Not Yet Scaled
--------------------------------------------------

This is the answer for a fully built platform that is real, deployable, and credible, but does not yet have major revenue scale.

Reasonable strategic value range:
- About $3.0M to $6.5M

Why that range makes sense:
- It is above pure replacement cost because the domain model and product definition already encode a large amount of business logic
- It is still capped because there is no proven revenue multiple yet
- A buyer would still need to fund go-to-market, onboarding, integrations, and customer success

My base-case opinion for this stage:
- Around $4M to $5M is a credible middle-band strategic value if the platform is genuinely production ready

--------------------------------------------------
10. Commercial Valuation With Early Traction
--------------------------------------------------

Scenario assumption:
- 8 to 15 paying enterprise customers
- Blended ARR per customer: $120,000 to $220,000
- Resulting ARR: about $0.96M to $3.3M
- Likely revenue multiple for a niche vertical platform: about 4.0x to 5.5x ARR

Implied value range:
- About $8M to $18M is the most practical early-traction range

Why not use much higher multiples:
- The marine dealership market is real but narrower than broad horizontal SaaS
- Customer concentration risk will be higher
- Implementation depth may keep services mix high early on
- Buyers will look hard at retention, gross margin, and deployment repeatability

--------------------------------------------------
11. Mature Niche-Platform Upside
--------------------------------------------------

Scenario assumption:
- Strong product-market fit in marine dealership and marina operations
- ARR in the rough range of $4M to $8M
- Real retention, strong integration stickiness, and repeatable onboarding
- Revenue multiple in the rough range of 4.5x to 5.5x ARR

Implied value range:
- About $20M to $45M

This is the upside case, not the default case.

You only reach this level if the platform becomes a genuine operating layer for revenue, service throughput, retention, and financial control rather than remaining a very smart internal planning tool.

--------------------------------------------------
12. Why the Project Has Real Value
--------------------------------------------------

This project has more value than a normal prototype for several reasons:

- The operating model is deep and specific to the dealership domain
- It spans revenue, service, finance, marina, retention, integrations, and KPI control
- It already demonstrates a multi-view product concept, not just one page
- Exportability and state persistence show a path toward operational use
- The front-end prototype reduces product-definition time and de-risks future design work
- The domain mapping itself is reusable IP

In plain business terms:
This is closer to the front edge of a vertical operations platform than to a generic visualization mockup.

--------------------------------------------------
13. Why the Value Is Still Discounted
--------------------------------------------------

The valuation should still be discounted for these reasons:

- No visible production backend yet
- No live customer contracts shown
- No recurring revenue shown
- No proof yet of enterprise deployment repeatability
- No measured retention, usage, or ROI metrics yet
- The marine vertical is specialized, which lowers TAM versus broader dealer software markets
- Integration-heavy platforms can be expensive to service if productization is incomplete

In plain business terms:
The idea and prototype are strong, but the market will pay much more for revenue and stickiness than for concept quality alone.

--------------------------------------------------
14. My Final Opinion
--------------------------------------------------

If this project is fully built out into a serious production platform, the most practical way to think about value is:

- Full build cost: about $2.0M to $4.8M
- Practical base-case build budget: about $3.1M
- Strategic asset value once truly production ready: about $3.5M to $6.5M
- Value with early commercial traction: about $8M to $18M
- Strong upside if it becomes a sticky niche platform: about $20M to $45M

If you want the shortest possible answer:
- A serious full build is probably a $3M-class project
- A fully built and credible version could reasonably be a $4M to $6M asset before scale
- With recurring revenue and real adoption, it can move into the low eight figures and potentially beyond
