Executive Position

This platform should not be valued like a simple diagramming interface. The current asset already contains a meaningful operating model, a reusable domain graph, multiple end-user views, export behavior, state persistence, and a clear roadmap toward a real vertical SaaS or enterprise operating layer.

Most Defensible Short Answer

If fully built into a production-grade marine dealership operations platform, the project likely represents roughly $2.0M to $4.8M in full build cost, with a practical base-case budget near $3.07M. Once production ready, but before strong recurring revenue, it reasonably supports a strategic asset value of about $3.5M to $6.5M.

Commercial Upside View

Once paying customers, repeatable onboarding, and real ARR exist, the valuation should move from replacement-cost logic to recurring-revenue logic. That is the shift that opens the path toward $8M to $18M in early traction, and potentially $20M to $45M if the product becomes a durable category platform.

Scenario Drivers and Operating Assumptions

The forecast ranges above are not arbitrary. They depend on customer mix, retention, implementation burden, and how embedded the platform becomes inside the operator workflow.

Conservative Driver Set

  • Smaller accounts or single-point operators
  • Longer sales cycles and narrower rollout scope
  • More services-heavy implementations and lower ARR density
  • Gross retention roughly 88% to 90%
  • Recurring-value proof takes longer to establish

Base Driver Set

  • 2 to 4 rooftop or operating-unit deployments
  • Recurring adoption across finance, service, marina, and retention use cases
  • Moderate implementation revenue with rising software mix
  • Gross retention roughly 90% to 93%
  • Operator dashboards and workflow controls drive stickiness

Upside Driver Set

  • Multi-site groups expand seat, workflow, and reporting footprint
  • Product becomes part of daily operations, not just oversight
  • Expansion revenue and premium modules raise contract value
  • Gross retention roughly 93% to 95%
  • Platform starts looking like category infrastructure within its niche

Strategic Buyer and Investor Lens

Different buyers will value this asset differently. Strategic buyers care about time-to-market, IP depth, and embedded workflow advantage. Financial buyers care more about repeatability, margins, retention, and concentration risk.

Potential Strategic Buyer Types

  • Marine dealer software vendors looking to expand into workflow and analytics
  • Marina or waterfront operations software providers seeking dealership adjacency
  • Dealer-management or ERP players wanting a modern operating overlay
  • Private-equity-backed platform consolidators building marine retail technology suites

What Raises Strategic Price

  • Unique domain model that would take time to rebuild
  • Demonstrated operational ROI in revenue, service throughput, or financial control
  • Clean integration architecture and deployable connectors
  • Referenceable customers and low churn in a specialized market

Why the Current Asset Already Commands Real Value

The project already contains more than mockup value because it encodes the operating model itself: demand, sales, F&I, service, parts, marina revenue, retention, analytics, governance, and third-party systems are already structured in one product concept.

Why the Valuation Still Gets Discounted

No visible live backend, no active customer contracts, no measured ARR, and no proven enterprise deployment repeatability. The market pays far more for demonstrated stickiness than for concept quality alone, even when the concept is strong.

Final Opinion

The cleanest way to talk about this project is to separate build cost, strategic asset value, and scaled commercial value. As a production-ready platform, it is best framed as a $3.5M to $6.5M strategic asset with a realistic build requirement near $3.07M. With real paying customers and repeatable deployment, it can move into the $8M to $18M band, and if it becomes a sticky marine-operations category platform, the five-year upside can reasonably push into the $20M to $45M range.
